Millions of children in Madagascar are suffering

In Madagascar, 47% of children aged 5 to 17 are involved in child labor; 37% of children are married before the age of 18; and 63% of children aged 1-14 have experienced corporal punishment (WHO). With 23 million people and half of the population being under the age of 18, we are talking about millions of children who are concerned.

In Elevatus, we strive to protect these children by giving them the tools they need to end the vicious cycle of generational poverty.

Child Labor

Child labor deprives children of their childhood, their potential and their dignity

Child labor refers to work that: is mentally, physically, socially or morally dangerous and harmful to children; and/or interferes with their schooling by: depriving them of the opportunity to attend school; obliging them to leave school prematurely; or requiring them to attempt to combine school attendance with excessively long and heavy work.
