The Junior School of Ambohidranomanga

The creation of our school

In 2018, thanks to joint efforts of the Elevatus board in Utah and the selfless sacrifices of our donors, we have restored a century old abandoned junior school in the village of Ambohidranomanga, and built 2 new classrooms, a teacher’s office, and a cafeteria. We also provide sewing classes and tutoring classes, as well as recreational activities. We hope to expand the range of extracurricular activities we offer, like cooking classes, danse and music activities, and so on.

Why we built it

Most of the children in Ambohidranomanga were forced to work in the nearby rock-quarry of Ambohibato to help their families. Since their parents could not afford to lose this source of income and let their children go to our new school, we started a sponsoring program to pay for their tuition fees and to provide two meals each day.

Still a lot to do

As of today, 60 of the students are sponsored, but there are 190 others who are still waiting for your help. You can change a children’s life by choosing today to sponsor one or more children. Not only will you give them a chance to education and decent food, but you will also be able to stay in touch with them through the efforts of our dedicated team on-site.
